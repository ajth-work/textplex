# Non-Romanized Language Learner Ecosystem and Domestic Literacy Anchors

This document is a planning aid for vocabulary selection.

Use it to answer two questions at the same time:

1. What resources do learners actually cluster around?
2. What does a domestic learner normally need by the end of school in that language community?

The goal is not to mirror every exam or curriculum in full. The goal is to bias TextPlex vocabulary toward what is both useful and widely encountered.

## Working Rule

Choose vocabulary from the overlap of:

- domestic school literacy goals
- exam-prep and placement ecosystems
- high-frequency native reading material
- the books and media learners actually consume

When those sources disagree, prefer the source that most strongly predicts sustained reading behavior for the target learner.

## Japanese

### Learner resources people gather around

- JLPT study material and level-based vocabulary lists
- school textbooks and kanji study charts
- manga, light novels, and literary fiction
- graded readers and learner-friendly news
- dictionary apps and sentence-mining tools

### Domestic literacy anchors

- Preschool: oral language growth, hiragana/katakana awareness, and comfort with books and labels
- Elementary: the grade-by-grade kanji allocation table, with 1,026 school-taught kanji by the end of grade 6
- Middle school: standard written Japanese, the common-kanji system, and broader reading of literary and academic prose
- High school: fluent reading of standard prose, newspapers, essays, and literature; practical command of the common-kanji system
- College: discipline-specific register, academic prose, and source-text reading across fields

### TextPlex implication

For Japanese, prioritize:

- common school and daily-life vocabulary first
- kanji compounds that recur in novels, essays, and newspapers
- readings that support mixed kanji/kana text without forcing romanization

## Chinese

### Learner resources people gather around

- HSK vocab lists and test-prep books
- school textbooks and classroom word lists
- graded readers, news, subtitles, and web fiction
- classical primers and curated literary corpora

### Domestic literacy anchors

- Preschool: oral language, story familiarity, and early awareness of characters and pinyin
- Elementary: the 3,500-character common-character base used in education, with roughly 1,600 recognized in early grades and around 2,500 writable by the end of elementary
- Middle school: continued expansion to the full common-character set, stronger writing, and more classical and argumentative reading
- High school: sustained reading of modern and classical prose, formal writing, and interpretation of literature and media
- College: academic prose, domain vocabulary, citation-heavy reading, and genre-specific terminology

### TextPlex implication

For Chinese, prioritize:

- the 3,500-character education core
- the vocabulary clusters around school texts, news, and common literature
- pinyin and segmentation support as a reading aid, not as a replacement for character literacy

## Korean

### Learner resources people gather around

- TOPIK prep and level-based vocab lists
- webtoons, web novels, subtitles, and drama transcripts
- school textbooks and graded reading materials
- newspaper and news vocabulary lists

### Domestic literacy anchors

- Preschool: spoken Korean, early print awareness, and pre-literacy activities
- Elementary: Hangul mastery, basic reading/writing, and standard orthography
- Middle school: fluent reading of unvoweled standard text, vocabulary expansion, and expository reading
- High school: argument writing, literature, media literacy, and academic register
- College: formal writing, research reading, and discipline-specific language

### TextPlex implication

For Korean, prioritize:

- high-frequency Hangul word forms
- school and media vocabulary over niche slang
- support for unvoweled text, spacing, and morphologically rich word forms

## Russian

### Learner resources people gather around

- TRKI / TORFL-style exam prep
- школьная программа texts, grammar drill books, and literature lists
- classic novels, contemporary fiction, and news media
- orthography and punctuation references

### Domestic literacy anchors

- Preschool: oral language and early awareness of the Cyrillic alphabet
- Elementary: fluent Cyrillic decoding, spelling basics, and sentence writing
- Middle school: orthography, punctuation, literature study, and text analysis
- High school: essay writing, literary analysis, and exposure to formal and journalistic prose
- College: academic style, domain vocabulary, and research reading

### TextPlex implication

For Russian, prioritize:

- orthographic accuracy and lemma support
- high-value literary and journalistic vocabulary
- morphology-aware lookup rather than surface-form-only matching

## Hebrew

### Learner resources people gather around

- YAEL-style placement prep
- ulpan materials and immigrant-language support resources
- school curricula, children’s literature, and newspapers
- religious and classical texts where relevant to the learner audience

### Domestic literacy anchors

- Preschool: oral language, early print awareness, and story vocabulary
- Elementary: letter recognition, vowel-mark learning, and movement toward unvoweled reading
- Middle school: reading fluency without niqqud, grammar, and text comprehension
- High school: literature, current affairs, formal essays, and advanced reading
- College: academic Hebrew, discipline vocabulary, and source-text interpretation

### TextPlex implication

For Hebrew, prioritize:

- unvoweled reading competence
- common school and media vocabulary
- careful handling of niqqud as an optional aid rather than the default display layer

## Arabic

### Learner resources people gather around

- school curricula in Modern Standard Arabic
- reading-comprehension and calligraphy materials
- Quranic and classical-text study resources
- newspapers, children's books, and language competitions
- Arabic-language proficiency and literacy programs

### Domestic literacy anchors

- Preschool: oral MSA exposure, story listening, and letter familiarity
- Elementary: decoding, spelling, short-vowel reading, and basic composition
- Middle school: unvoweled MSA reading, morphology, and formal written expression
- High school: literature, essay writing, media reading, and more advanced formal registers
- College: academic prose, rhetoric, and field-specific terminology

### TextPlex implication

For Arabic, prioritize:

- Modern Standard Arabic vocabulary first
- morphology and derivation support
- right-to-left rendering and diacritics handling as core requirements

## Practical Ranking Rule

When TextPlex needs to choose between two candidate vocabulary sets, rank them in this order:

1. Curriculum vocabulary used by domestic learners
2. Vocabulary that appears in official exam prep
3. Vocabulary that appears in the learner community’s most-used reading media
4. Domain-specific literary vocabulary

That ordering keeps the app aligned with what learners are most likely to need soon, while still leaving room for aspirational reading material.
